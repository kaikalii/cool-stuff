Tourist Destinations

This page is here to show you all the top places that you have to go on your trip to Greece

Acropolis of Athens

4.8 stars(145.5k) Historical landmark Closed Athens, Greece

This iconic 5th-century B.C. citadel towers over the capital city of Athens. Walk up the limestone hill to explore the majestic ruins of the Parthenon Temple, the Erechtheion, and the Temple of Athena Nike.

The Acropolis Museum

Located just a short walk down a pedestrian promenade from the hill, this ultramodern glass-and-steel facility houses thousands of priceless artifacts. Transparent glass floors allow you to peer down directly into the excavations of an ancient Athenian neighborhood below. Explore exhibits and buy tickets in advance on the Official Acropolis Museum Website.

Mainland Wonders

Meteora Monasteries

4.9 stars (53.3k) Monastery Kalabaka, Greece

Located in central Greece, Meteora features an otherworldly landscape of towering sandstone rock pillars. Perched precariously on top of these cliffs are six active, medieval Eastern Orthodox monasteries. You can climb up stone steps carved directly into the rocks to view breathtaking Byzantine frescoes and panoramic valley views.

Archaeological Site of Delphi

Ancient Sanctuary

Situated on the scenic, dramatic slopes of Mount Parnassus, Delphi was considered the "navel of the world" by ancient Greeks. Pilgrims from across the ancient Mediterranean traveled to the Sanctuary of Apollo to seek prophecy from the mystical Oracle of Delphi. The site includes a stunning ancient theater, treasuries, and an athletic stadium.

Ancient Mycenae

4.7 stars (21.6k) Archaeological site Closed Mykines, Greece

Located in the Peloponnese region, this prehistoric acropolis dates back to the Bronze Age (12th century B.C.). Pass through the famous monumental Lion Gate to explore the legendary kingdom of Agamemnon, complete with massive "Cyclopean" stone walls and ancient vaulted tombs.

World-Famous Islands

Santorini Caldera

4.6 stars (532) Volcano

Known for its dramatic volcanic cliffs, whitewashed houses, and bright blue domes, Santorini is a premier island destination. Travelers flock to the village of Oia to witness its world-famous sunsets, hike the scenic trail from Fira, or take boat cruises around the volcanic bay.

Crete

The Giant of the South Open Crete, Greece

Greece's largest island is celebrated for its immense variety. Highlights include the romance of Chania Old Town, the ancient Minoan Palace of Knossos, and world-renowned exotic coastlines like the pink sands of Elafonisi.
